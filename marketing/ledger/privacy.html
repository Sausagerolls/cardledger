Privacy Policy

CardLedger · Last updated 17 June 2026

CardLedger is made by Giant Mushroom Studio ("we", "us"). This policy explains what happens to your information when you use the CardLedger app. The short version: your collection stays on your device and in your own iCloud. We run no servers and collect nothing about you.

What we collect

Nothing. We have no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking. We do not receive your cards, photos, prices, or any usage data.

Where your data lives

  • On your device. The cards you log, their photos, prices and settings are stored locally on your iPhone or iPad.
  • In your iCloud. If iCloud is enabled, that same data is backed up and synced through your private iCloud account so you don't lose it. This is handled by Apple under Apple's Privacy Policy. We cannot see it.

Card database look-ups

When you use auto-fill to search for a card, the app sends only your search term (for example a card name or number) directly to the relevant third-party card database to fetch matching results and images. No personal information is sent. Depending on the game, that service may be:

  • Scryfall (Magic: The Gathering)
  • YGOPRODeck (Yu-Gi-Oh!)
  • pokemontcg.io (Pokémon)
  • A community Dragon Ball Fusion World dataset hosted on GitHub

These services have their own privacy policies. If you never use auto-fill, the app makes no such requests.

Camera & photos

The app asks for camera and photo-library access only so you can attach pictures of your cards. Those images are stored with your inventory (on device and in your iCloud) and are never sent to us.

Desktop (local network) mode

When you turn on Desktop mode, the app runs a small web server that shares your inventory with browsers on the same local network only, while the app is open. It is not exposed to the internet, and nothing is sent to us. Turn it off and the server stops.

Children

CardLedger is a tool for collectors and traders and is not directed at children under 13.

Your rights

You are in control of your data at all times. Delete a card to remove it; delete the app to remove its local data; manage or delete the iCloud copy through your device's iCloud settings. As we hold no data about you, there is nothing for us to delete on our side. Under UK GDPR you retain your data-subject rights with respect to data held in your own iCloud by Apple.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update this page and the date above.
